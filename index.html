Pollutants

._____________________________________________.

The data to the right is an organized and graphed interpretation of the "Marine Microplastic Concentrations", tracked by the Nation Oceanic and Atmostpher Administration (NOAA). It reports the 160000+ cases of microplastic concentrations in 3 oceans, the Atlantic, Pacific, and Arctic. These graphs indicate not only the abundant amount of microplastic in our main oceans, but also how more than half of the cases have quite a severe amount.

While the manufacturing industry continues to grow, so do the mounds of plastic and waste that wash up on shores and endanger animals. Without recognizing, we've put our world in harmsway for us and the terrestrial animals, finding it harder and harder to find a clean source of water, and especially for the marine animals, who fall victim to the waste and oil spills.

The 6 charts above are from an Australian company that tracked the reported Pollutant Spills that occured near Queensland, Australia. Even from one section of the world, there were more than 200 REPORTED cases in the span of 3 years. These charts conclude that ships have been the #1 source of the polutant spills and they typically spill diesel and other forms of oil.[ The other listed in the graphs indicate more specific oils and forms of metals and garbage]

Harm on the Animals

._____________________________________________.

While you can easily find some of the animals in aquariums and possibly zoos, some people tend to forget that some of those species, in addition to hundreds more, are actually threatened or endangered species. Admired and highly adored animals like whales, certain types of sharks, manatees, dolphins, sea otters, certain types of seals, and several kinds of fish and coral and running the risk of being extinct!

Do your research!

Although this data set is not that big, it speaks and still reflects the data on many other charts. It shows that while some conservation methods do make cases better, there are still several other species that decline. That information is indicated by the graph on the right which specifies if certain conservation movement for each species benefited the group or did the opposite. A MAJORITY of cases got worse.

This set includes several more cases and indicates what the group who gathered the data focused on. It also tells us how land animals, like birds, have also been severly affected, as well as the fish and animals inside the water. The text below is the same data set which shows there are more than 1300 species that are in trouble.
